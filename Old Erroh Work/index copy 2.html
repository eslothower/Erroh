Cellular Calls

By using iPhone Cellular Calls, you can answer a phone call on your
Mac, iPad, or iPod Touch when on the same Wi-Fi network as your phone.

Requirements

  • An iPhone running iOS 8.1 or later
  • An activated carrier plan associated with your iPhone
  • A Mac running MacOS Yosemite or later
  • An external microphone or headset when using a Mac mini or Mac Pro
  • A separate iPhone, an iPad, or an iPod touch running iOS 8 or later

Set Up Cellular Calls

  • Are your devices logged into iCloud with the same Apple ID?
  • Are your devices signed into FaceTime with the same Apple ID?
  • Are both devices connected to the same network through either Ethernet or Wi-Fi?
  • Is Bluetooth enabled on both of your devices?
  • On your iPhone, go to Settings > Phone > Calls on Other Devices, and enable Allow Calls on Other Devices.
  • On your iPad or iPod touch, go to Settings > FaceTime, and enable Calls from iPhone.
  • On your Mac, go to the FaceTime app and click FaceTime > Preferences in the menu bar. Then click Settings and enable Calls from iPhone.

Solutions

  • On all of the devices you want iPhone Cellular Calls to work on, go to Settings > General > Handoff > and toggle Handoff off and back on. Restart those devices.
  • If you're using it on your Mac, go to System Preferences > General, and enable Allow Handoff between this Mac and your iCloud devices.
  • Also on your Mac, go to FaceTime > Preferences and enable iPhone Cellular Calls. If it was already enabled, toggle it on and off again. Restart your Mac.
  • If these solutions did not work, contact Apple Support through online chat or call them at (800)–692–7753, 7 days a week from 7:00 a.m. to 10:00 li.m. Central time.

Auto Unlock

If you're wearing your Apple Watch, your Mac will know you're nearby and will automatically unlock itself for you.

Requirements

  • An Apple Watch running watchOS 3 or later
  • Any kind of Mac from mid-2013 or later running macOS Sierra 10.12 or later

Set Up Auto Unlock

  • If this is your first time using Auto Unlock after you turned off, restarted, or logged out of your Mac, you will need to manually enter your password. From then, until you turn off, restart, or log out of your Mac, your Apple Watch will unlock your Mac for you.
  • Is your Apple ID using two-factor authentication and not two-step verification?
  • Is Bluetooth enabled on your Mac?
  • Is your Mac connected to Wi-Fi?
  • Are both of your devices logged into iCloud with the same account?
  • Is your Apple Watch using a passcode?
  • Is Internet Sharing disabled on your Mac?
  • Click the Apple logo in the menu bar > System Preferences > Security and Privacy > General tab and make sure Allow your Apple Watch to unlock your Mac is enabled.

Solutions

  • Toggle Bluetooth on and off on both devices.
  • Toggle Wi-Fi on and off on both devices.
  • Restart your Apple Watch by holding down the Digital Crown and side button at the same time until you see the Apple logo.
  • Restart your Mac
  • Check for updates. On your iPhone go Watch app > My Watch tab > General > Software Update. If there is an update, tap Install.
  • On your Mac, click the Apple logo in the menu bar > About This Mac > Overview tab > Software Update. If a software update pops up, press Update.
  • Repair your Apple Watch by going to the Watch app on your iPhone. Click the My Watch tab > [Name]'s Apple Watch > Info (looks like an "i") > Unpair Apple Watch. Repeat the paring process. Reenable Auto Unlock on your Mac by clicking the Apple logo in the menu bar > System Preferences > Security and Privacy > General tab and make sure Allow your Apple Watch to unlock your Mac is enabled
  • If these solutions did not work, contact Apple Support through online chat or call them at (800)–692–7753, 7 days a week from 7:00 a.m. to 10:00 li.m. Central time.

Personal Hotspot

By using your Personal Hotspot on you iPhone, other devices can connect to it to use network features.

We are currently working on solutions to Personal Hotspot through Bluetooth connection and USB connection. At the moment, we only have solutions for Personal Hotspot through Wi-Fi connection. We are sorry for the inconvenience.

Requirements

  • To enable Personal Hotspot, you need an activated carrier plan providing Personal Hotspot service with one of these devices running iOS 8.1 or later:
  • An iPhone 5 or later
  • iPad 4 or later
  • Any generation of iPad Mini
  • To connect to a Personal Hotspot, you need one of these devices running iOS 8 or later or, with a Mac, MacOS Yosemite or later:
  • An iPhone 5 or later
  • iPad 4 or later
  • Any generation of iPad Mini
  • iPod Touch (5th generation) or later
  • Any Mac from 2012 or later

Set Up Personal Hotspot

  • Is Personal Hotspot enabled on your iPhone or iPad (Wi-Fi + Cellular) in Settings > Cellular > Personal Hotspot?
  • Are your devices logged into iCloud using the same Apple ID?
  • Is Bluetooth enabled on both of your devices?
  • Are your devices connected to Wi-Fi?

Using Personal Hotspot

  • On your Mac, click the Wi-Fi icon in the menu bar and choose the name of the Personal Hotpot you'd like to connect to.
  • On your iOS device, go to Settings > Wi-Fi > and tap the name of the Personal Hotpot you'd like to connect to.
  • If it prompts you to enter a password: on your device that is providing the Personal Hotspot, go to Settings > Personal Hotspot > Wi-Fi Password, and see what password it is displaying. That is the password it's asking you for.

Solutions

  • Restart the iOS device providing the Personal Hotspot. Then restart the device(s) using the Personal Hotspot.
  • Check for updates on all of the devices providing and using the Personal Hotspot.
  • On the iOS device providing the Personal Hotspot, go to Settings > General > Reset > Reset Network Settings.
